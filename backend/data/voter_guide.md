# 🛰️ CivicPulse Intelligence Guide

## 1. Voter Eligibility in India
Every Indian citizen who is 18 years of age or older on the qualifying date is eligible to be enrolled as a voter. Enrollment must be done in the part of the electoral roll of the constituency where the person ordinarily resides.

## 2. The Voting Process (EVM & VVPAT)
India uses a highly secure Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) system.
1. **Identification**: Present your EPIC (Voter ID) or one of the 12 alternative documents.
2. **Ink Marking**: The second polling officer will mark your left forefinger with Indelible Ink.
3. **The Ballot**: Inside the voting compartment, press the blue button on the Balloting Unit next to your chosen candidate's symbol.
4. **Verification (VVPAT)**: Look at the VVPAT glass. A slip will be visible for 7 seconds showing your chosen candidate's symbol before dropping into the sealed box.

## 3. Common Misconceptions in Indian Elections
- **[MISCONCEPTION]**: You can vote if you only have a Voter ID card.
  - **TRUTH**: Your name MUST be in the Electoral Roll. Having an ID card is not enough; enrollment is mandatory.
- **[MISCONCEPTION]**: You can take your mobile phone inside the booth.
  - **TRUTH**: Mobile phones, cameras, and recording devices are strictly prohibited inside the polling booth to maintain secrecy.
- **[MISCONCEPTION]**: Pressing the button twice counts as two votes.
  - **TRUTH**: The EVM locks after the first vote is cast and only resets for the next voter after the Presiding Officer enables it.

## 4. Fact-Check & Myth-Buster (Debunking Misinformation)
- **Claim**: "The ink used in elections can be washed off with lemon juice."
  - **FACT**: The Indelible Ink contains Silver Nitrate, which reacts with the skin to create a permanent mark that cannot be removed by any chemical or solvent. It only disappears as new skin grows.
- **Claim**: "Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) can vote online from abroad."
  - **FACT**: Currently, NRIs must be physically present at their polling station in India to cast their vote. Online or proxy voting is not yet enabled for general NRI voters.
- **Claim**: "If I press NOTA, the election is canceled if NOTA gets the most votes."
  - **FACT**: Even if NOTA (None of the Above) receives the highest number of votes, the candidate with the next highest number of votes is declared the winner. NOTA is a way to express dissent but does not cancel the election results.
